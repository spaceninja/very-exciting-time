No UFO files released this time around, but we’ve got plenty of other exciting news. Jeremy Corbell revealed that ODNI agents, while trying to smear Dylan Borland, revealed the name of a UFO program: Project Rubik’s Cube. Meanwhile, Lue Elizondo was asked about secret military hybrid alien breeding programs, and he confirmed he heard about them during his time at the Pentagon. Ross Coulthart and others continue to claim that the White House is reaching out to religious leaders and UFO community members to prepare for possible disclosure of contact with nonhuman intelligences. And UAP whistleblower David Grusch will speak at a bipartisan press conference in the nation’s capital to advocate for whistleblower protections and the passage of the UAP Disclosure Act.

## Project Rubik’s Cube

- UFO journalist Jeremy Corbell, on stage at the McMinnville UFO Fest, along with fellow journalist George Knapp and UAP whistleblower Dylan Borland: “As a journalist, George and I have been exposed to certain things. I talked with our lawyer in the hotel right before we came here. I was told, by an ODNI agent, that Dylan testified to ICIG, the police of the intelligence agencies, that there is a UFO program, and it is called Rubik's Cube.”
- Borland, while Corbell is talking, is leaning away a little nervously, and as soon as Corbell says the name of the program, he leans back, says “wow” and rubs his chin nervously. And the longer Corbell talks, the more uncomfortable he looks, until he has fully leaned forward, with his head in his hand.
- Corbell: “That’s what I heard from the ODNI. They shouldn’t be calling me and telling me what was described in a SCIF, should they? I’m a journalist, so I listen… Project Rubik’s Cube. Dylan, I’m not even gonna look at you.” _(audience laughter at how nervous Dylan looks at the question he knows is coming.)_ “Have you ever testified to ICIG about a UAP program — containing UAP — called Project Rubik’s Cube?”
- Borland: “Let me collect my thoughts…” _(audience reactions: “No!” “Don’t do it!”)_ “I am not in a SCIF. I am not going to jail. I can neither confirm nor deny, but if you want me to answer that question, ask for amnesty for the whistleblowers, ask Congress and the Executive Branch to give it to us, and I'll answer that question, gladly.” (fist bump with Corbell)
- Skip Atwater, the former head of Project Stargate, the Pentagon’s remote viewing program: “It’s hard to talk about dimensions, but I was standing behind an alien in a spacecraft… He said, ‘you shouldn’t be here.’ And I said, ‘I know, I realize this is a strange place, but before I go could I ask you a question?’ And he said, ‘okay.’”
- Atwater: “I said ‘How do you get from here to Alpha Centauri? We can’t do that. The only thing that we think about is, I have to go really fast for a really long time. How is it that you can go to Alpha Centauri without any problem?’ And essentially, he said, ‘you wouldn’t understand.’ So I said, ‘I agree that’s a problem.’”
- Atwater: “However, I know that you have access to my mental thoughts. I mean, you’re not talking to me with your mouth… Can you use what information I have in my head and try to explain it to me?’ And he said, ‘imagine a Rubik’s Cube, but not necessarily just the six sides, just imagine the concept of a Rubik’s Cube… So, what you do to get to Alpha Centauri is you just twist the cube.’”

## Hybrid Breeding Program

- Celebrity podcaster Jillian Michaels, interviewing former head of AATIP Lue Elizondo: “Where do we start? Should we start with an alien hybrid breeding program…?”
- Rep. Matt Gaetz, a few weeks ago: “I had someone come and brief me, who was in a military uniform, worked for the US Army… on the locations of hybrid breeding programs, where captured aliens were breeding with humans to create some hybrid race that could engage in intergalactic communications…”
- Rep Gaetz: “He was a senior enlisted officer… He came into my office… in a non-classified setting. I had members of my staff there, and what they explained is that the military ran a very secret program where aliens, that were living, were in forced breeding programs with humans that had been abducted from war zones and even the caravans of migrants.”
- Rep Gaetz: “Now, again, I didn’t verify this, but what the whistleblower was telling me was that there were between six and twelve locations around the country where this happened, and what he wanted was for members of Congress to all show up at the same time at all of these different locations so any of those activities could not be moved. And of course, it’s a physical impossibility to get members of Congress to show up eight locations at one time, and so that never occurred.”
- Elizondo: “Now, as far as this notion of alien crossbreeding and hybridization, let me just preface this: when I was in the Pentagon at AATIP, I was a nuts-and-bolts kind of guy, so really airframe, air integrity, the materials science and propulsion behind the vehicles themselves, not so much the occupants. What Gaetz is referring to is something called, in the vernacular, throughout the UFO community, MILABs.” (Military Abductions)
- Elizondo: “What it would suggest is that there's been a long-standing agreement or understanding between us and our friends from out of town that have allowed us to work with whoever whatever these are in a cooperative agreement. In exchange for that, supposedly they would have access to certain human specimens biologically in order to test… I have to say, first and foremost, I heard about that while I was at the Pentagon. I was not privy to the specifics of that. From my understanding, that relationship began, if it is true, sometime in the 1950s and has continued.”
- Elizondo: “I have met several individuals, former intelligence officers for the Air Force and other services, who swear by it and actually say they've had encounters with UAPs and have seen military personnel in and around the vehicles, as if very nonchalant, as if it was supposed to be… I've had plenty of individuals tell me verbatim that the military… is far more involved in this topic than anyone really knows. There are cases which seem rather interesting where you have… UAPs potentially being escorted.”
- Former CIA analyst John Ramirez, four years ago: “Hybridization. That's the word that Lue Elizondo can't say that everyone in the Pentagon is expecting him to say someday.”
- Elizondo: “Let's get back to the question… about… alien hybrids… I have to be careful with going into any type of speculation about hybridization or any type of scientific study or research because that was not my forte. What I will say is… there are people out there that swear by it. There are people that have been in the military and intelligence officials that have told me that, who I have no reason to question their credibility. They've never lied to me, certainly, but I don't have firsthand knowledge of that hybridization or forced hybridization.”
- Elizondo, in another interview: “There were people in my organization that were definitely focused on it,” he says. “And I’m not going to sugarcoat it. They were. They were convinced that there was some sort of interference, if you want to call it that, of human evolution at some point in the past. Whether that’s a genetic genome that has been changed or purposely mutated, or that is some sort of influence, I don’t know. That was never my area of expertise and I am only repeating what I heard.”
- UFO journalist Walter Kirn, responding to the Jillian Michaels interview: “This is no joke, my sources tell me. Highest-level briefings have been held, all the way to the top.”

And I know how it was controlled… So here's one of the loopholes they use… White House Special Access Programs. But there's… a way to develop a White House SAP that is non-covert action. And so that was one of the tricks of the trade, burying it over in the Department of Energy, but also keeping it in these non-covert action programs that five people on the NSC know about and the president at one time.”

- Coulthart: “What I thought was most significant about this was that Steven Miller, the deputy chief of staff for the White House and somebody who's very close to Trump, Burlison’s saying Miller reached out to _him_… So basically, Burlison floated this idea of an amnesty previously, and Miller was thinking about it. Now, the fact that the White House Deputy Chief of Staff is thinking about this, actually mentally engaged with this subject matter, I think is incredibly significant. That to me was the biggest positive that I took away.”
- Coulthart: “What Representative Burlison is proposing is this 60-day window for insiders with knowledge of or involvement in UAP crash retrievals, reverse engineering, or related programs, and that they can feel comfortable about coming forward safely and providing information. This was framed as a potential mechanism to encourage disclosures while offering protections aligning with broader efforts to encourage and advocate for UAP transparency under the current administration… That confirms what I’m being told privately, that the White House is wrestling with a decision about whether or not to disclose.”
